Faith: As a religious man, Defoe incorporated his views on divine providence and the importance of faith in God, highlighted in Crusoe's spiritual transformation throughout the novel. Colonial Relations: Defoe's experience as a merchant and his knowledge of colonial enterprises are evident in Crusoe's interactions with indigenous peoples, reflecting the colonial and Eurocentric attitudes of the time.
